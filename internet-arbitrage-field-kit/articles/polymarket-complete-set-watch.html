Internet Arbitrage Field Kit

Polymarket complete-set watch

Prediction-market prices can look like an arbitrage when the displayed probabilities do not add up neatly. This scanner uses public market data and public orderbooks to check a narrower question: can every mutually exclusive outcome in one market be bought at current best asks for less than 1.00?

Latest included scan

Example Command

.\scan-polymarket-complete-sets.ps1 -Proxy http://127.0.0.1:10808 -MaxMarkets 120 -MinLiquidity 250 -MinVolume 500 -MinEdgePerSet 0.005 -MinExecutableEdgeUsd 0.25 -ExportCsv latest-polymarket-watch.csv -ReportPath latest-polymarket-watch.md

How To Read A Row

  • Price Sum is only the displayed market-data sum. It is a clue, not an executable trade.
  • Ask Sum is the sum of current best asks from orderbooks. Below 1.00 is the only long-only complete-set condition this scanner treats as a possible edge.
  • Top Depth is the smallest best-ask size across outcomes, which limits how much of the displayed edge might be available before prices move.
  • Edge At Top is still before fees, slippage, leg risk, settlement risk, and account eligibility checks.

What It Does Not Do

  • No orders, signatures, private keys, or fund movement.
  • No attempt to bypass location, account, or platform restrictions.
  • No guarantee all legs can fill atomically.
  • No legal, tax, settlement, or jurisdiction advice.
