# Internet Arbitrage Map

Goal: find legal online spreads where the resale, bundle, or output value is higher than the acquisition, tooling, platform, and time cost.

## Best Low-Complexity Categories

### 1. Digital Product Bundle Arbitrage

Create a bundle from assets you own or can legally use, then sell the bundle for more than the value of its parts.

Examples:

- Templates plus an offline HTML tool.
- Free sample plus paid bundle.
- Public-domain material plus original formatting, notes, and tools.

Avoid:

- Reselling copyrighted files.
- Copying paid templates from other creators.
- Fake scarcity or fake income claims.

### 2. Marketplace Fee Spread

Same product, different platform economics.

Examples:

- Sell a digital download on a no-listing-cost platform first.
- Use GitHub as a public sample page, then move serious checkout to Payhip, Ko-fi, Gumroad, or another platform later.
- Compare platform fees, payment fees, fixed fees, and refund risk before choosing price.

Key math:

`Net = revenue - platform fees - payment fees - fixed fees - support/refund reserve`

### 3. Attention Arbitrage

Convert useful free content into traffic for a paid download.

Examples:

- Free checklist page.
- Free template samples.
- Public GitHub README.
- Searchable how-to article.

This is slow but cheap. It is one of the few paths with no required ad spend.

### 4. API Or Data Packaging Arbitrage

Use public or licensed data, then add formatting, alerts, summaries, or workflow value.

Examples:

- Local calculator for spread math.
- CSV tracker that turns raw prices into net profit decisions.
- Report template for a niche buyer.

Avoid:

- Scraping sites against their rules.
- Selling raw data you do not have rights to redistribute.
- Promising trading profit.

### 5. Crypto Spot Spread Observation

Look for differences between markets, but count every cost.

Costs:

- Trading fees.
- Spread/market impact.
- Withdrawal fees.
- Network fees.
- Deposit/withdrawal delays.
- Price movement while funds move.
- KYC/account limits.

Use this kit to observe and calculate. Do not trade only because a raw spread looks positive.

### 6. Affiliate/Referral ROI Arbitrage

Promote a product where payout exceeds content/ad cost.

Examples:

- Free guide that includes a compliant affiliate link.
- SEO page with comparison table.
- Tool page that naturally recommends a paid platform.

Avoid:

- Spam.
- Fake reviews.
- Hiding affiliate relationships.
- Claims you cannot prove.

### 7. Service-To-Template Arbitrage

Turn a repeated service workflow into a downloadable tool.

Examples:

- Quote calculator.
- Proposal template.
- Client email pack.
- Invoice/receipt generator.

This avoids live custom delivery and fits the online-only constraint.

## Fast Filter

Good opportunity:

- Legal rights are clear.
- No live fulfillment required.
- Fees are measurable.
- Buyer pain is specific.
- Net profit remains positive after all costs.
- Distribution channel exists.

Bad opportunity:

- Depends on account abuse.
- Depends on spam.
- Depends on moving money through risky platforms.
- Looks profitable only before fees.
- Requires customer support you cannot provide.
- Uses content you do not own or cannot license.

## $100 Math

- $39 product: 3 sales = $117 gross.
- $29 product: 4 sales = $116 gross.
- $19 product: 6 sales = $114 gross.
- $9 product: 12 sales = $108 gross.

For most low-traffic internet-only paths, higher-priced bundles are easier than very cheap products.
